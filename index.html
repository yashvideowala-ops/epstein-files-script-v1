SECTION 5: THE 2008 PLEA DEAL - A SYSTEM FAILURE (12:30 - 15:30)

[VISUAL: Palm Beach Police Department exterior]
NARRATION:

In March 2005, Palm Beach police began investigating Jeffrey Epstein after a mother reported that he had sexually abused her 14-year-old daughter.
[VISUAL: Police report documents]
The investigation quickly expanded. Federal officials identified 36 underage victims. Some as young as 14. The FBI opened "Operation Leap Year" in May 2006.
[VISUAL: FBI building]
By May 2007, federal prosecutors had drafted a 60-count indictment and an 82-page memo detailing federal sex crimes. Epstein was facing decades in prison.
[VISUAL: Court documents, indictment pages]
But then something extraordinary happened. U.S. Attorney Alex Acosta, head of the Southern District of Florida, overruled his own prosecutors.
[VISUAL: Photo of Alex Acosta]
On September 24, 2007, Acosta's office signed a non-prosecution agreement with Epstein's legal team. The deal was unprecedented.
[VISUAL: NPA document on screen]
Epstein would plead guilty to two Florida state charges: soliciting prostitution and procuring a minor for prostitution. He would serve 18 months in a county jail—not a federal prison—with work release privileges. He served just 13 months.
[VISUAL: Palm Beach County jail exterior]
In exchange, the federal investigation was dropped. And here's the most shocking part: the agreement granted immunity not just to Epstein, but to all unnamed co-conspirators.
[VISUAL: Immunity clause highlighted in document]
The victims were never consulted. They were never informed. According to a 2020 Justice Department review, prosecutors actively concealed the deal from victims until after Epstein had pleaded guilty.
[VISUAL: Victim advocate press conference]
In 2019, the 11th Circuit Court of Appeals ruled that prosecutors had "misled" victims and possibly engaged in "active misrepresentation." The deal violated the Crime Victims' Rights Act.
[VISUAL: Court ruling document]
But by then, it was too late. Epstein had served his time. He was free. And for over a decade, he continued his life among the elite.
